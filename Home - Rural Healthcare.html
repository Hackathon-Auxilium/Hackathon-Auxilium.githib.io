- Arun Maira

‘Access to healthcare’ is not only a necessity, but our government’s promise and every individual’s wish. Out of 65% of our rural population, which makes up 86% of the medical visitors, have to travel over 100 kms to reach a medical facility. With empty hands and empty pockets but a beating concern in their hearts, how far can they go?

The Healthcare System in India can be summed up in one word: Insufficiency. Insufficiency of access, insufficiency of affordability, insufficiency of inclusivity. Due to the lack of availability in Public Hospital facilities, even rural folks take the road of the Private. Private Hospitals having the prices doubled or non-coupled, many walk away with sighs. Either because they wouldn’t be able to pay for it, or those who do it out of their pockets, are pushed to the ledge of poverty post procedure. As per the study conducted by IMS Institute for Healthcare Informatics, poor patients receiving outpatient care for chronic conditions at a private facility spend on average 44% of their monthly household expenditure per treatment, against 23% for those using a public facility. Furthermore, in rural areas the difficulty in accessing transport is another major concern. When traveling becomes the only mode of travel for the ill, we could only imagine what it is like being in their shoes. The concept of health literacy, which is defined as the degree to which individuals have the capacity to obtain, process, and understand basic health information needed to make appropriate health decisions, remains unexplored in rural areas. As their beliefs and customs either overpower the study of science, or they are unable to get a proper knowledge of the topic as barely any educational institution is present or available. A country as big, diverse and widespread as India, has to make its healthcare its foundation, for the people. For every person that dies due to lack of healthcare. For the family of the person who suffers the loss. For everyone who is trapped by the barrier of money.
